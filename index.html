🌙 Solunar Theory
Astronomical
Solunar theory was developed by John Alden Knight in 1926 after observing that fish and game activity peaked at predictable times related to the moon's position. It's been refined over nearly 100 years and is used by serious anglers and hunters worldwide.
MAJOR PERIODS (2hrs)
Occur when the moon is directly overhead or underfoot (transit and anti-transit). Gravitational pull is strongest — triggers instinctive feeding response. Two per day, ~12hrs apart.
MINOR PERIODS (1hr)
Occur when the moon is at 90° offset to transit (moonrise and moonset). Secondary gravitational influence. Less powerful than majors but still worth targeting, especially when overlapping with dawn/dusk.
Best combination: Major solunar period + incoming tide + dawn or dusk = maximum fish activity. When all three align, that's your target window.
